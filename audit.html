Section 01 · Typography & voice

Editorial type, not landing-page type

The piece sets every word in Helvetica. Investigative journalism almost always uses a serif for headlines and a fixed-width font for figures — it signals "real reporting" without saying anything.

01

Set the headlines in a serif

All views

Sans-serif headlines on a piece this serious read as marketing. A tight news serif (Source Serif, Lyon, or just plain Georgia) gives the headline editorial weight. The big "Once a publicly financed candidate…" intro paragraph would also become serif, not Helvetica.

Affects: every scene heading, the after-scrolly intro paragraph, the "Drag the slider" subhead, and the article-body paragraphs.
Now
Montgomery County Public Campaign Financing Example
Proposed
Montgomery County Public Campaign Financing Example

02

Use a fixed-width font for every dollar amount

All views

When the slider moves and numbers tween, proportional digits jump around because each glyph has a different width. A fixed-width digit font keeps the columns rock-steady and signals "this is data" the second the reader sees it.

Affects: every dollar amount in the chart, the big "Donation amount $100" / "Candidate gets $650" callouts, the equation tier rows, and the "$870,170" max-county callout.

$50+$350=$400
Proposed

Section 02 · Color & texture

The current palette is loud

The "prohibited donations" panel is a rounded card with a soft red border. The "max county money" callout is a smaller rounded card. Editorial layouts almost never use cards — they use horizontal rules, indents, and small-caps headers to do the same separating work.

Affects: the area immediately above the headline in the article body.

Section 05 · Charts & annotations

The chart is the article. Treat it like one.

Cairo's whole thing is that an information graphic should explain itself. The current chart has labels stacked next to bars, no leader lines, no annotations.

17

Add explainer annotations connected by leader lines

All views

When the slider crosses $50, a small annotation should fly in: "Tier 1 fills first — it's matched 7-to-1, so $50 of donor money pulls $350 of county money." Connected to the bar with a thin leader line. Single highest-leverage change to make the piece actually teach.

Affects: the bar chart in the after-scrolly slider section AND the bar chart inside scenes 1–5 of the scrollytelling.
Now
x7 / Tier 1 / $350 — three labels floating around the bar with no explanation.
Proposed
Tier 1 fills first. The first $50 a donor gives is matched seven-to-one — that $50 pulls $350 of county money.
Apply this change?

Section 06 · Mobile & portrait

Mobile is now spacing-correct, but still small and clipped

After the latest fix the portrait stack reads heading → list → chart → equation top-to-bottom without overlaps, but the chart itself takes only about a sixth of the screen height. Two structural moves below.

20

Make the bar chart bigger on mobile

Mobile only

The chart is the visualization — make it the most prominent thing on screen. Currently on phones it's about 120 pixels tall (the size of a thumb). Scaling it up to fill 60% of viewport height is the biggest legibility win available without rewriting the chart.

Affects: scenes 1–5 of the scrollytelling on phones (and the after-scrolly slider chart, secondarily).
Now
~120 px tall on a 375-wide phone.
Proposed
~190 px tall — chart fills its band.
Apply this change?
21

Drop the sticky-stage on mobile, use linear full-screen scenes

Mobile only

Sticky stages on phones are awkward — the address bar steals viewport, the user can't see what's coming, the scroll feels stuck. Editorial mobile scrollytellings often skip stickiness on phones and let scenes flow as full-screen sections that fade in as the reader enters them.

Affects: the entire scrollytelling section on phones — every scene becomes a discrete vertical section instead of being painted over a single sticky stage.
Now
Sticky 80vh stage on mobile. Reveals trigger via scroll. The reader's thumb scrolls over a "stuck" graphic for the entire scrolly.
Proposed
On phones only: each scene is its own full-viewport section. As the reader scrolls into it, the scene fades in and the next one fades out below it. Page scrolls naturally.
Apply this change?
22

Bring back the full scene-6 comparison on mobile, redrawn

Mobile only

The latest fix simplified scene 6 on mobile by hiding the bottom row (the "ten $10 donors → $1,150" half of the comparison). The reader now only sees one half of the "what's better" question. Better fix: keep both halves but redraw the comparison as a vertical stack — one $100 donor at top, ten $10 donors at bottom, both flowing right into "becomes $X to the candidate".

Affects: scene 6 layout on phones only.
Now
On mobile, scene 6 shows only one half: $100 donor → $650. The "ten $10 donors → $1,150" comparison is hidden.
Proposed
Both halves, stacked vertically. Top: $100 donor → $650 candidate. Bottom: 10 × $10 donors → $1,150 candidate. Reader can compare directly.
Apply this change?
23

Bigger touch target on the slider thumb (mobile only)

Mobile only

The current slider thumb is 22 pixels — fine on a desktop trackpad, hard for a thumb on a phone. The visible thumb can stay small, but the actual hit-target should be at least 44 pixels (Apple HIG / Google Material guidance).

Affects: the donation slider in the after-scrolly section, on phones only.
Now
22-pixel circular thumb. Fine on desktop. On a phone, finger slides past it constantly.
Proposed
Visible thumb stays the slim square (per item 9), but its hit area on phones is 44×44 — meets accessibility guidelines and feels solid under a thumb.
Apply this change?
24

Tighten tablet-portrait spacing

Tablet only

In between the desktop landscape layout and the phone portrait layout, the tablet (roughly 769–1024 px wide) inherits desktop spacing — which leaves a lot of side margin and a too-tall sticky stage. A dedicated tablet pass with a slightly narrower content column and 80vh stage uses the screen better.

Affects: the entire piece at tablet-portrait width (iPad Mini portrait, iPad portrait).
Now
Tablet inherits desktop layout. Sticky stage is 80vh = ~820 px on a 1024-tall tablet. Lots of empty side margins.
Proposed
Dedicated tablet rules: max 720-px content column, 75vh stage, tighter heading paddings, single-column equation table.
Apply this change?
Section 07 · Spacing & whitespace

Use the vertical space, don't waste it

Several spots have luxurious padding that just leaves the reader with empty grey space. Editorial layouts run tighter — information density is a journalism virtue.
