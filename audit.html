Section 01 · Typography

Editorial type, not SaaS-landing-page type

The current piece sets headlines in Helvetica Neue, body in Helvetica, all numbers in Helvetica. Investigative journalism almost always uses a serif for headlines and a mono for figures — it signals "real reporting" without saying anything.

01

Set the headlines in a serif

Both

Sans-serif headlines on a piece this serious read as marketing. Switch to a tight news serif (Source Serif 4, Lyon, or just plain Georgia) so the headline carries editorial weight. The big "Once a publicly financed candidate…" intro paragraph would also become a serif body, not Helvetica.

Montgomery County Public Campaign Financing Example
Proposed
Montgomery County Public Campaign Financing Example

02

Use a mono font for every dollar amount

Both

When the slider moves and the numbers tween, proportional digits jump around because each glyph has a different width. A mono digit font (JetBrains Mono, Roboto Mono, or IBM Plex Mono) keeps the columns rock-steady AND signals "this is data" the second the reader sees it.

Right now there's no section structure — the reader floats from "intro" to "tier mechanics" to "interactive slider" with no signposting. CNS Maryland uses small-caps section labels with a horizontal rule. Adds zero pixels of clutter and makes scanning trivially easier.

Section 02 · Color

The palette currently looks like a crypto onboarding deck

#229500 / #0027ff / #7600ff are juicy. They're saturated to within an inch of their lives. Editorial scrollytellings tend to use muted, slightly-desaturated versions of the same hues so the chart's color doesn't out-volume its content.

Section 04 · Layout

Borders, cards, and rounded boxes everywhere

The "prohibited donations" panel is a rounded card with a soft red border. The "max county money" callout is a smaller rounded card. Editorial layouts almost never use cards — they use horizontal rules, indents, and small-caps headers to do the same separating work.

Section 05 · Charts & data viz

The chart is the article. Treat it like one.

Cairo's whole thing is that an information graphic should explain itself. The current chart has labels stacked next to bars, no leader lines, no annotations, no in-chart explanations of why you're looking at what you're looking at.

16

Add Cairo-style annotations with leader lines

Both

When the slider crosses $50, a small annotation should fly in: "Tier 1 fills first — it's matched 7-to-1, so $50 of donor money pulls $350 of county money." Connected to the bar with a thin leader line. This is the single highest-leverage change to make the piece actually teach.

Current
x7 / Tier 1 / $350 — three labels floating around the bar with no leader lines or explanation.
Proposed
Tier 1 fills first. The first $50 a donor gives is matched seven-to-one — that $50 pulls $350 of county money.

Section 06 · Mobile

Mobile is now legible but still has wasted space

After the v2.11 fix the portrait stack reads heading → list → chart → equation top-to-bottom without overlaps, but the chart is still ~120 device px tall — small for the most important visual on the page. Two structural moves below.

19

Scale the bar chart 1.6× on mobile so it fills its band

Mobile

Wrap the chart elements in a group at boot, then apply scale(1.6) on mobile-portrait so the chart takes ~190 device px tall instead of ~120. This is the biggest legibility win available without rewriting the chart geometry.

Current
Chart at ~120 device px tall — readable but the slider's purpose (compare bar areas as $) gets lost.
Proposed
Chart at ~190 device px tall (60% bigger) by wrapping chart elements in a <g transform="scale(1.6)"> at mobile boot.
Apply this change?
20

Consider non-sticky linear flow on mobile

Mobile

Sticky stages on phones are awkward — the address bar steals viewport, the user can't see what's coming, the scroll feels stuck. Editorial mobile scrollytellings (e.g. Pudding, Reuters Graphics) often skip sticky on phones and let scenes flow as full-screen sections. Keeps reveals scroll-driven, drops the "stuck" feel.

Current
Sticky 95vh stage on mobile. Reveals trigger via scroll. Reader's thumb scrolls over a "stuck" graphic for the entire scrolly.
Proposed
On mobile only: stack scenes as discrete full-viewport sections, one after another. Each section reveals on enter (intersection observer). Page scrolls naturally; no stickiness.
Apply this change?
